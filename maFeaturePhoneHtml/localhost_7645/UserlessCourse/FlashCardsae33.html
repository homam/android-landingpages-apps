Can anger turn positive?
I was angry with my friend I told my wrath, my wrath did end. I was angry with my foe; I told it not, my wrath did grow. William Blake 1757-1827

Anger can serve a positive function when expressed properly. Studies show that 'anger can have beneficial effects on individuals' health, their relationships and their work.'

Socially, very positive changes can derive from anger. For instance, the civil rights movement of the 1960s and the abolishment of slavery or the women's suffrage movement in the early 20th century.
