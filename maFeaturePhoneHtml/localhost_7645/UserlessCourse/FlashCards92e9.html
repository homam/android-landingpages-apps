Be visible

A good performer who isn't visible is probably just as likely not to be promoted as someone who doesn't perform as well, but is highly visible. One sure way to manage your career is to make sure that you are visible to the right target audience inside your organisation. Important question here is: how to get visible?

Interact with management

Someone at a higher level has a better understanding of how your job and department fits with the goals and mission of the company. They are also better informed on any new and upcoming initiatives and strategic goals. Having more conversations with the high flyers provide you with insight as to where you might fit in the greater scheme of things.

Get feedback

Although your immediate boss provides you with periodic feedback on your work, it's important to obtain feedback that is geared toward future development rather than past performance.

Spotlight your achievements

Are you working on a project that is of interest to upper management? Although you are probably updating your manager with progress reports, a CC on an email to his manager highlighting important achievements would be a great way to spotlight you. In situations where this might be inappropriate, ask your manager if you should CC upper management.

Volunteer

Is there a project of particular interest to upper management where your expertise would be welcome? Working with different people in different departments provides you with an excellent opportunity to gain visibility and showcase your skills and talents in a productive collaborative way.

Be creative

Is there something you know that can be improved upon? Whether it's creating greater efficiency, finding new ways of applying information, or new tools that could be useful, the bigger your vision, the greater your visibility with the decision makers.
