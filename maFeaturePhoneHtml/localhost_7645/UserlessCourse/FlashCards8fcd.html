9. Say sorry

Despite all the useful tips we have given, there is no doubt you will get angry. Many parents worry that apologising will undermine their authority with their children. Not true because:

An apology serves several purposes:
  • It helps restore positive feelings between you and your child.
  • It reduces the amount of resentment a child may feel because of your outburst.
  • An apology gives you a chance to talk about why everyone was so upset and to discuss what is expected instead.
  • It teaches children how to behave when they make a mistake.
