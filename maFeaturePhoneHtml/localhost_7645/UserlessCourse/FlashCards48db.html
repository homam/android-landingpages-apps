How to handle your anger

So you know that you frequently lose it at the office. Knowing what triggers your anger is the place to start. Next, recognise when a trigger is coming to the surface and decide how you want to respond. Let's check out these tools:

Count to 10

Before reacting to a stressed situation, take a few moments to breathe deeply and count to 10.

Confide in a colleague

The next time your boss really gets under your skin, talk to a colleague. He/she might be able to help you put things back in perspective.

Take a break

If things get really intense at work - let's say the promotion you have been looking out for is given to a colleague who took credit for all your work - don't dwell on it and remove yourself from the situation. Grab a coffee or take the afternoon off. Whatever you do, make sure to leave the office to blow off some steam.

Joke about it

Joking about a tense situation can help you ease the tension and can put things back in perspective.

Forgive

We know this is a difficult one. Try to forgive people instead of holding a grudge. This will lead to more happiness and will make life lighter.
