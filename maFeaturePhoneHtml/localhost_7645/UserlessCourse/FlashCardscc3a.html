Listen!
Courage is what it takes to stand up and speak; courage is also what it takes to sit down and listen. Winston Churchill 1874-1965

Without listening skills, you do not get a sense of what team members think of the projects they work on. Read the given tools to become a good listener:

  • Stop talking This includes the voice inside your head. Focus on the person speaking and keep eye contact.
  • Try to see the other's point of view
  • Don't interrupt
  • Observe non-verbal behaviour Pick up unspoken messages and pay attention the rate of speech, facial expressions and eye contact.
  • Give feedback via non verbal clues Like head nods, leaning forward and eye contact. Avoid jumping in with evaluative, critical remarks. This causes withdrawal or defensiveness.
  • Check for understanding Rephrase what you think the other person has said.
