Know how to handle conflicts

Being a leader, you have to know how to handle employees that don't do their work properly. Confront people directly with the issue. If there is a conflict between others you need to know how to manage the situation effectively. Here are some tips to help you.

Listen to both sides

Make sure you hear both sides of the aggrieved parties before you can decide what to do next to help resolve the conflict.

Do your own research

What really happened? Go out of your way to find out what the real problem is. Before you say or do anything, make sure that you are 100% sure of the complete story.

Get to the root

Ask yourself whether the conflict is the real problem. Can't it be an underlying issue? This is often the case and may cause resentment and frustration. These negative emotions can result in a conflict. Knowing the underlying issues can help you end the problem.

Use diplomacy and tact

Even if you know that one party is at fault, do not confront them with it in public. Instead, deal with them in private so that they are not embarrassed and humiliated. Your team must work in harmony once the conflict is resolved, so keep your eye on the long term when trying to handle arguments and problems. Some problems are petty and can usually resolve themselves. As a leader, you don't have to step in and take care of every single problem that your team faces.
