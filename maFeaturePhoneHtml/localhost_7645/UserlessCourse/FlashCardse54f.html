Overview
To listen well is as powerful a means of influence as to talk well, and is essential to all true conversation. Chinese proverb

Research suggests that we're not very good listeners at all. Apparently we only remember about 25-50% of what we hear. The best listeners almost always turn out to be the best negotiators. Why? They observe both the verbal and non-verbal communication skills of their counterparts. They pay close attention to other negotiators and register how they use word choice, sentence structure and vocal skills like pitch and speed rate for effect.

Explore the five ways to improve your listening skills that will contribute to making you a better negotiator.
