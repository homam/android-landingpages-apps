Close your speech

One of the main issues that public speakers face is that they don't know when to stop. Every person, be it the President of some state or a school captain has a limited time to present their speech. When they exceed it, not only do they make the audience restless but they are remembered as the 'person whose speech went on too long.'

So finish on time.

You can close your speech in a variety of ways - with a joke or witty statement, something serious or thought provoking. It should sound like the natural conclusion of your presentation.

Leave the audience with something to talk about. Be thought provoking.
