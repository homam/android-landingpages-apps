Structure your presentation

Every successful presentation has a structure that tells a story. Create a story around your presentation that will grab the attention of your audience and get your message delivered. A compelling presentation has a beginning or the 'opening', a middle or the 'body' and an end or the 'closing'. If you keep that in mind, you'll have a clear idea as to what you want to say, when you should say it and how to tie the threads of your presentation together.

1. Opening

A good opening line sets the tone for the rest of your presentation. It can be a story, a video, a question, something humorous or even a shocking statistic. Think of how this opening will get the attention of the audience.

2. Body

Identify the three most important bits of information and bind them in a pattern that is easy to understand and hard to forget. Work on the 'rule of three' and create a balanced, informative and credible presentation.

3. Closing

The way you close your presentation is your last chance to make an impact on your audience. Reinforce your points, remind the audience about your opening statements and relate them to your ending. For example, if you started with an explosive statement, correlate it with your ending to make a point.
