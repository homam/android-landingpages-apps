Consequences

Punish the behaviour, not the emotions. For example, if your child tries to hit you, punish in the way that is appropriate to what they have done immediately. But if the child does stomp into his room, screaming at everyone standing in his way, it is recommendable not to confront him with this emotional outburst until he has calmed down. Kids get angry just like adults; they need to feel that sometimes it is okay to let off steam. As long as they're not crossing your lines, give them time to be angry.
