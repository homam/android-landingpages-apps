Overview
Usually when people are sad, they don't do anything. They just cry over their condition. But when they get angry, they bring about a change. Malcolm X 1925-1965

Everyone feels angry sometimes. It is in our genes to become angry every now and then. Humans in the stone age needed to become angry when they felt trapped or hurt. Nowadays, living in a 'concrete age', we still become angry when we feel mistreated. This can lead to destructive and violent behaviour, and causing trouble in relationships.

We associate anger with physical, mental and social challenges. In this chapter we show that you can deal with anger in a constructive and healthy way.

Martin Luther King, Gandhi, Malcolm X and Mother Theresa - are all people who were angry about racial injustice, occupation of their country and poverty - changed their anger into constructive action.
