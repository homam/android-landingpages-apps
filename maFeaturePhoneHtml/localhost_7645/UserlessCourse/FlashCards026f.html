Placing horizons

Before you position a horizon, you need to [{(cundefined)}]think about what it is you want to have in your image. Ask yourself whether it is the ground or the sky that you want to put into focus.

For example, if you want to capture the shadow of a long caravan in the desert during sunset, give dominance to the ground (two thirds of the photo). On the other hand, if you see a flock of birds on their migratory path, then give dominance to the upper portion of the picture.
