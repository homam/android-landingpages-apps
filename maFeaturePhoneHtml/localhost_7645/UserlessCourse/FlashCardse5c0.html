Recognise the triggers

Listed below are the common triggers for a child's tantrums together with some possible advice to help you calm your child. Know that expressing anger is healthy and normal, even for children. You can't always protect them from feeling sad or angry, but you can help by finding patterns in your child's bad behaviour and making adjustments that will cause less drama.

Just before dinner time

Advice: Take 15 minutes together to relax and talk over the day before you begin making dinner. Give your child something relaxing to do while you are preparing dinner. A healthy snack may also keep them busy and avert hunger which can fuel anger.

Changes

Advice: Children need routine and structure. Take a couple of minutes each morning to talk about the activities of that day. If there is a change, take five to ten minutes before this moment and tell the child what the change will be.

Over stimulation

Advice: Try not to over-schedule. Avoid certain activities if your child is sensitive to stimulation.

Tiredness

Advice: Make sure your child is getting enough sleep (10-12 hours a day). Develop a bedtime routine to prepare the child for bed.

Jealous feelings

Advice: Let the child know that feeling jealous sometimes is perfectly normal. Try to focus on the strengths your child has and in case there is a sibling, never compare them and try to spend some alone time with each of them.

Child doesn't get own way

Advice: Pick your battles. If it's important to you, stick to your guns. If your child doesn't listen, apply a consequence. You can also present the child two choices she/he can select from. This allows the child to feel some sense of control.

Too much energy

Advice: Allow some time to your child each day to run and jump and let off steam in a positive way.
