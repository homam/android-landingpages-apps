Internal sources of anger

Anger comes from our irrational perceptions of reality. There are four types of internal reasoning that contribute to anger:

1. Emotional reasoning

People who reason emotionally, misinterpret normal events and innocent remarks as being threatening; they perceive it as an attack on themselves.

2. Low frustration tolerance

Stress-related anxiety lowers tolerance for frustration and we begin to see normal things as threats to our well-being or threats to our ego.

3. Unreasonable expectations

The frustration tolerance will lower when people make demands, see things how they should be and not how they really are.

4. Dehumanising by labelling

Here, we apply a derogatory label upon someone else. By thinking of someone as being an ' idiot' , it dehumanises them which makes it easier for them to become angry at this person.
