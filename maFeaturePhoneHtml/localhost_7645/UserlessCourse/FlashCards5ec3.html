How is anger expressed?

People are different and so is the way they express themselves. There are three general types of anger expression:

  1. Aggressive anger Focused at the other person to hurt him/her intentionally. This can be emotionally or physically. Yelling, humiliating and using your fists are examples of aggressive anger.
  2. Passive anger Avoid dealing with the situation that causes feelings of anger. This anger can be expressed by getting even, holding a grudge and being mean. Forms of passive anger are: spreading nasty rumours, giving the silent treatment, getting others to turn against someone etc...
  3. Assertive anger Here the anger is expressed directly and in a non-threatening way to the person involved. A statement such as "It makes me feel angry when you ...", is an example of assertive anger.
