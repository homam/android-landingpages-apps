How to handle an angry partner

Try to practice following techniques with your partner:

  • Chill out and think. Cool off, take a time out, gather your thoughts and stand in other person's shoes. Try it and you will see that this will result in having a clearer understanding of the situation.
  • Keep calm Anger feeds anger, so the calmer you remain, the quicker your partner's anger will disappear.
  • Acknowledge your partner's feelings Just say that you know that your partner is angry.
  • Show that you're listening Use active listening techniques using non-verbal communication like nodding so that your partner sees that you're listening and feels heard.
  • Make peace (not war) Show that you want to make peace. Don't be stubborn and say sorry if this is appropriate.
  • The art of responding Try to respond effectively by communicating your feelings and needs. Say things like: "It really hurts me when you do..." or "It would mean a lot to me if you would..." Remarks like these are more likely to de-emphasise the angry state of mind your partner is in.
If one of you is still unable to control his/her bad temper, it is wise to seek professional help.
