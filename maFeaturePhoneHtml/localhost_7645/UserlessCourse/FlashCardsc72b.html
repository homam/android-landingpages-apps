Twitter

With Twitter, you can use 140 characters max to post your thoughts, 'retweet' other peoples thoughts or post links to articles or pictures you want to share. When you're looking for a job, Twitter can be your friend. Used in combination with other social media sites like LinkedIn and job search engines, Twitter can help you market yourself.

Here some strategies for using Twitter to job search:

The first impression

You never get a second chance to make a first impression so spend time creating a profile with as much information as possible.

Create your own brand

In your online biography, use keywords and phrases relevant to your industry to attract the attentions of recruiters.

Follow the right stuff

Follow relevant companies and individuals. It goes without saying that following relevant companies and top players in the industry you want to be working in, will keep you informed of anything also relevant to you.

Be a professional

Participate in talks and discussions, follow the latest trends, and give your professional opinion on certain matters in your field.

Subscribe to relevant lists

Lists which can be found on your profile page can be a useful feature to find the most relevant users to follow in your field. If your Tweeted content is relevant, eventually you will be added to specific lists by other users to find.

# !

Hashtags are used to connect specific topics. For job searches you can for instance use: #jobs, #jobadvise, #jobsearch, #careers, #dreamjob etc...
