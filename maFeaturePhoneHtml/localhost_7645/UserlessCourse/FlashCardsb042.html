Research

Verify a contractor's experience and credentials before making any hiring decisions. If you don't mind working with a less experienced contractor, you might be able to get a lower price.

You can put yourself in a better bargaining position if you conduct a thorough research on the material costs used in the project. Keep this list with you to know if the contractor is charging you more than the market rates.

Good idea Find out if your contractor has other jobs scheduled and where your job falls on his list of job priorities. When a contractor juggles multiple projects, yours can get delayed.
