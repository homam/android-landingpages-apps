Practice

Even if you do all the preparation in the world, it won't help you on the presentation day until you 'practice'. If you memorise, you may sound mechanical, too polished, error-free and unapproachable to the audience. Start practicing at least 15 days before your presentation and you'll sound as if you're conversing with the audience about a topic that you've mastered.

  • Practice out loud in front of a mirror.
  • Videotape yourself to have a better idea of your body language.
  • Practice in front of someone else. Ask for their feedback.
  • Practice first in sections and then when you feel you have mastered each section practice it in it's entirety.
  • Once you have practiced it enough times you will feel more comfortable with the material and will be able to come across as more natural in your presentation.
